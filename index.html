Research

01

Vocational Training for South Sudanese Refugee Youth in Uganda

with Denni Tommasi (University of Bologna, JPAL, IZA) and Till Wicker (Tilburg University)

Registered RCT

This project studies the effects of a 9-month vocational training program for young South Sudanese refugees in Uganda. The intervention combines residential training, formal certification, apprenticeship, and a start-up kit, and the study tracks labor market outcomes, migration decisions, and well-being over repeated follow-up surveys.

02

When Information Reallocates Opportunity: A Two-Sided Incentivized Experiment with Young Workers

with Denni Tommasi (University of Bologna, JPAL, IZA) and Till Wicker (Tilburg University)

Registered RCT

This project studies apprenticeship-market frictions in West Nile, Uganda, through a two-sided experiment with firms and young workers. It compares two information regimes: one that reveals personalized worker-firm fit and one that reveals congestion in area-location cells, with the aim of understanding how information shapes beliefs, targeting, matching, and access to opportunities.
